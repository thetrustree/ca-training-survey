Why the class is built this way
01 The sale is lost before the table, not at it. Four out of five said process or confidence. Nobody said price. The fix lives in mindset and discovery, not in a better closing script.
02 The class opens with belief, not technique. Four out of five named mindset and self-worth as the biggest gap. If a CA doesn't believe they're worth what they charge before they walk in the door, the skills don't matter.
03 Objections are a process failure. All five pointed the same direction. Prevent first, handle second. Run the process right and most objections never show up.
04 Role play is non-negotiable and needs to be treated like the real thing. Unanimous on reps. Joey added the most important note: CAs treat role play like practice where they expect to mess up. That ends in this class.
05 One thing needs to be aligned before we build. Andre sees CAs leading with financing naturally. Three others say it only comes out when the homeowner hesitates. We teach it one way or we teach it wrong. That conversation happens before August 17.
