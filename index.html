What this tells us about the class
01 The sale is lost before the table, not at it. Four out of five said process or confidence. Nobody said price. The fix lives in mindset and discovery, not a better closing script.
02 The class opens with belief, not technique. Four out of five named mindset and self-worth as the biggest gap. If a CA doesn't believe they're worth what they charge before they walk in the door, the skills don't stick.
03 Objections are a process failure. All five pointed the same direction. Prevent first, handle second. Run the process right and most objections never show up.
04 Role play is non-negotiable and needs to be treated like the real thing. Unanimous on reps. Joey named the problem: CAs treat it like practice where they expect to mess up. That changes in this class.
05 One thing needs to be resolved before we build. Andre sees CAs leading with financing naturally. Three others say it only comes out when the homeowner hesitates on price. We need to land on one answer before August 17.
